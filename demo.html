Digital India

Digital India is an initiative launched by the Government of India for connecting and empowering people in the field of technology. The initiative comprises of several projects which emphasize on delivering services digitally, digital infrastructure creation and digital literacy. From smartphones to lightning-fast laptops to GPS devices, it's hard to imagine life without technology. In the twenty-first century, one of the most important technologies is the power of the digitization. The system, which allows individuals to communicate globally. With Digital India project, the government is ready for the big programme by connecting every service with the e-power. This facility will aim to lessen the usage of physical documents and enable sharing of e-documents across organizations. . The e-power platform will facilitate more creative and service-oriented business models that create employment opportunities. High speed network will aid adequate infrastructure for online education platforms through smart and virtual classrooms. The geNext services like cloud helps in lowering the carbon footprint by reducing fuel consumption, waste management, greener workplaces and thus leading to a green computing. You can contribute to Digital India programme by your projects based on the following major area identified by government known as "Pillars of Digital india"
-> Universal Access to Mobile Connectivity
-> e-Governance - Reforming Government through Technology
-> Broadband Highways
-> eKranti - Electronic delivery of services
-> Public Internet Access Programme
-> Information for All
-> Electronics Manufacturing
-> IT for Jobs
-> Early Harvest Programmes

Make in India

The Make in India program was launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in September 2014 as part of a wider set of nation-building initiatives to encourage multi-national, as well as national companies to manufacture their products in India. The unprecedented 'Make in India' campaign aims to transform the economy from the services-driven growth model to labor-intensive manufacturing-driven growth. This will help in creating jobs for over 10 million people, who join the workforce every year. PM Modi has painstakingly identified 25 key sectors in which India can excel in world trade and manufacturing. These include -> Automobiles -> Automobile Components -> Aviation -> Biotechnology -> Chemicals -> Construction -> Electrical Machinery -> Food Processing -> Information Technology and Business process management -> Oil and Gas -> Pharmaceuticals, to name a few. You can use these ideas for developing your project and its field of application. We look forward to new and innovative ideas from you. We wish you all the best. Team Navarachanaa

Skill India

Skill India, an initiative launched by Government of India on 16 July 2015. The objective of this mission skill development is to enable Indian youth to become employable. This initiative creates scope, space and opportunities for the blooming of talent of Indian youth and providing training, support and guidance to for all occupation. The Objectives of the Skill India programme are:
-> To develop skills of the youth so that they become employable.
-> Create scope,space and opportunity for the youth.
-> Providing training,support and guidance for all occupations.
-> Rural India Skill Development
-> Mentorship support for the youths.
-> Building and Mobilisation activities.
-> To propose a structured and pragmatic solution to address the lack of relevant skills amongst the current and potential.
You can be part of this campaign by contributing in the following areas:
1. Training and support programmes
2. Rural India Skill development
3. Need based program
4. Building and mobilisation activities
5. Mentorship Support
